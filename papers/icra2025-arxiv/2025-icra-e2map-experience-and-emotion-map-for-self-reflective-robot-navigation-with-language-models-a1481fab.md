## TL;DR

- Large language models (LLMs) have shown significant potential in guiding embodied agents to execute language instructions across a range of tasks, including robotic manipulation and navigation.
- However, existing methods are primarily designed for static environments and do not leverage the agent's own experiences to refine its initial plans.
- Given that real-world environments are inherently stochastic, initial plans based solely on LLMs' general knowledge may fail to achieve their objectives, unlike in static scenarios.
