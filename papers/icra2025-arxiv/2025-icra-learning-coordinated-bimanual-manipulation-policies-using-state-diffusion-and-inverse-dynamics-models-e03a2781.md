## TL;DR

- When performing tasks like laundry, humans naturally coordinate both hands to manipulate objects and anticipate how their actions will change the state of the clothes.
- However, achieving such coordination in robotics remains challenging due to the need to model object movement, predict future states, and generate precise bimanual actions.
- In this work, we address these challenges by infusing the predictive nature of human manipulation strategies into robot imitation learning.
