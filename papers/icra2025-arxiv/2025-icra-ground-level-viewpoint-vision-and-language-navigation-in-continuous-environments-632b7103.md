## TL;DR

- Vision-and-Language Navigation (VLN) empowers agents to associate time-sequenced visual observations with corresponding instructions to make sequential decisions.
- However, dealing with visually diverse scenes or transitioning from simulated environments to real-world deployment is still challenging.
- In this paper, we address the mismatch between human-centric instructions and quadruped robots with a low-height field of view, proposing a Ground-level Viewpoint Navigation (GVNav) approach to mitigate this issue.
