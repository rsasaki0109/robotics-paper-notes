## TL;DR

- Partial hand amputations significantly affect the physical and psychosocial well-being of individuals, yet intuitive control of externally powered prostheses remains an open challenge.
- To address this gap, we developed a force-controlled prosthetic finger activated by electromyography (EMG) signals.
- The prototype, constructed around a wrist brace, functions as a supernumerary finger placed near the index, allowing for early-stage evaluation on unimpaired subjects.
