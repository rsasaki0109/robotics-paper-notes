## TL;DR

- Game-theoretic models are effective tools for modeling multi-agent interactions, especially when robots need to coordinate with humans.
- However, applying these models requires inferring their specifications from observed behaviors---a challenging task known as the inverse game problem.
- Existing inverse game approaches often struggle to account for behavioral uncertainty and measurement noise, and leverage both offline and online data.
