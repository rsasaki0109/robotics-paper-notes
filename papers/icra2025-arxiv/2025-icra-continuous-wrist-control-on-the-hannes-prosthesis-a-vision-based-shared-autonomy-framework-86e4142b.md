## TL;DR

- Most control techniques for prosthetic grasping focus on dexterous fingers control, but overlook the wrist motion.
- This forces the user to perform compensatory movements with the elbow, shoulder and hip to adapt the wrist for grasping.
- We propose a computer vision-based system that leverages the collaboration between the user and an automatic system in a shared autonomy framework, to perform continuous control of the wrist degrees of freedom in a prosthetic arm, promoting a more natural approach-to-grasp motion.
