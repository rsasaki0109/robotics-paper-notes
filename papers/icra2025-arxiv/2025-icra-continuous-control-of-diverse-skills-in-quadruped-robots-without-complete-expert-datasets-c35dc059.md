## TL;DR

- Learning diverse skills for quadruped robots presents significant challenges, such as mastering complex transitions between different skills and handling tasks of varying difficulty.
- Existing imitation learning methods, while successful, rely on expensive datasets to reproduce expert behaviors.
- Inspired by introspective learning, we propose Progressive Adversarial Self-Imitation Skill Transition (PASIST), a novel method that eliminates the need for complete expert datasets.
