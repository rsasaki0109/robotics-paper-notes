## TL;DR

- We consider the problem of indoor building-scale social navigation, where the robot must reach a point goal as quickly as possible without colliding with humans who are freely moving around.
- Factors such as varying crowd densities, unpredictable human behavior, and the constraints of indoor spaces add significant complexity to the navigation task, necessitating a more advanced approach.
- We propose a modular navigation framework that leverages the strengths of both classical methods and deep reinforcement learning (DRL).
